Existence of Geodesics via Heat Flow.

Posted by: Daniel B.
[2019-05-14 Tue]

Theorem Let \(M\) be a compact Riemannian manifold. Then every homotopy class of closed curves in \(M\) contains a geodesic.

The cruve \(\gamma : (a,b) \subset \mathbb{R} \to M\) is geodesic if and only if in locoal coordinates it satisfies

\begin{equation} \ddot{\gamma}^k(t) + \dot{\gamma}^i(t)\dot{\gamma}^j)(t)\Gamma_{ij}^{k}(\gamma(t)) = 0. \end{equation}

To apply the heat flow approach, we consider the following family of curves:

\begin{equation} u : S^1 \times [0,\infty) \to M, \end{equation}

where \(S^1\) is the unite circle, and the parabolic equation

\begin{equation} \begin{split} u_{t} &= u^k_{ss} + u^i_{s}u^j_{s}\Gamma_{ij}^k & s\in S^1, t\geq 0,\\ u(s,0)& = \gamma(s) & s\in S^1. \end{split} \end{equation}

The Heat Flow Method

  1. There exist \(T_0>0\) such that the initial value problem above has a solution \(u^k_{t} = u^k_{ss} + u^i_{s}u^j_{s}\Gamma_{ij}^k\) in \(S^1\times [0,T_0)\) and \(u(s,0)=\gamma(s)\), i.e., we have a short time existence of a solution. Then the maximal interval of existence of a solution is nonempty and open.
  2. Let \(u(s,t)\) be a solution. Get an apriori estimate of the spacial derivative \(|u_s| \leq C\), where \(C\) independently of \(t\). Then we can think of the equaiton \(u_t - u_{ss} =f\), where \(f\) is a bounded function. Then regularity theory for linear parabolic equations is used to contorll higher order derivatives which are time independent.
  3. This apriori estimate implies the following: If a solution exists in \([0,T_{0})\), and for \(t\to T_{0}\) the solution \(u(s,t)\) will converge to a smooth curve \(u(s,T_{0})\). Then this curve can be taken as the new initial value and continue the solution for times bigger than \(T_{0}\). Then the maximal interval of existence of a solution is also a closed set. This at the end implies that a solution exists for all times \(t>0\).
  4. Note that the energy \(E(u(s,t))\) is decreasing in \(t\) and since \(0 \leq E(u(s,t))\) for all \(t\), it is possible to find a sequence \(\{t_n\}\) such that \(u(s,t) \to \tilde{\gamma}(s)\), \(t\to \infty\), and \(\tilde{\gamma}\) is such that \(\tilde{\gamma}^k_{ss} + \tilde{\gamma}^i_{s}\tilde{\gamma}^j_{s}\Gamma_{ij}^k = 0\)
